Life Story

Based on true events

  • February 1979

    My Humble Beginnings

    Born and raised in St. John's Newfoundland, the farthest East you can go in North America before England.

  • 1985

    Commodre64

    When I was 6, my Dad was one of the first people in our area to get a computer for his work - a glorious Commodore64. I spent hundreds of hours learning how to program in C from my dad's books (and more hours playing Maniac Mansion cartridge)

  • 1991

    First Drum Kit

    My uncle gave me his old, dusty Ludwigs out of storage since he no longer played. I drove my parents and neighbours nuts.

  • 1997-2002

    Post-Secondary

    I initially went to university to study virology, but that didn't pan out. So I fell back on my interests - computers and programming.

  • 2006

    Move to Ottawa

    The .com bubble burst in Newfoundland, hard to come by IT jobs. Upon a whim, I decided to move to Ottawa. I packed my drums with my clothes, and my car with my drums, and set off.

  • 2008

    Canada Council for the Arts

    My first real influencial job. Managing a team of highly skilled developers taught me a lot - be humble, be simple, be smart.

  • 2018

    Solution Architect

    Learning all about Enterprise Architecture, and how to establish its practice.

History

On stage with RDP (1997)

I started playing drums when I was about 11 or 12 years old. I'm self-taught; I learned to play by watching drummers and listening to bands like Led Zeppelin.

I was gifted an electric guitar by my aunt in 2013, and I've been teaching myself how to play ever since by using resources like Ultimate-Guitar.com and YouTube.

I've since played in many bands, toured, and performed many shows and festivals.

Being a self-taught musician has been the core of my philosophy: that anyone can master anything, as long as the seat-time is put in. Spending thousands of hours in the studio honing my craft is my passion, and getting better is simply a byproduct of that effort.
